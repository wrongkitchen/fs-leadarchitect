Plaza 889
A retail environment drawing on the very culture of Shanghai
Creating a retail environment which is both in tune with and inspired by the rich culture of Shanghai.
The design of the mall was influenced by the contrasts between the two sides of the Huangpu river, Puxi and Pudong. Puxi is the more architecturally historic side and Pudong a hot bed of contemporary architecture. These architectural contrasts are explored in the material choices, and detailing of the two sides of the mall, with a lighter, steel and glass approach to one side, while the other enjoys a more solid aesthetic of stone, wrought iron and timber.
Project Facts
Client: Yueda Real Estate Development Company
GFA: 88,000sqm
Levels: 5
Designed: 2008 – 2009
Completed: 2010

Lead individuals in the Benoy Design Team, and their roles:
Simon Chua – Project director
Kenji Wong – Retail planning, architecture and Interior design

Team Credits
